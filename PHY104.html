Current and Resistance

Current (I) is the amount of charge per time 
that passes through an area perpendicular to the 
flow: 
        I = Q/t
Current is measured in SI units of amperes (A), 
and 
        1 A = 1 C/s
This definition for current can be applied to 
charges moving in a wire, in an electrolytic cell, 
or even in ionized gases.

DC Circuits

Definition: The closed path in which the direct 
current flows is called the DC circuit. The current
flows in only one direction and it is mostly used 
in low voltage applications. The resistor is the 
main component of the DC circuit.

AC Circuits

The circuit that is excited using alternating 
source is called an AC Circuit. The alternating 
current (AC) is used for domestic and industrial 
purposes. In an AC circuit, the value of the 
magnitude and the direction of current and 
voltages is not constant, it changes at a regular
interval of time.
